1st International Conference on Environment

Management, Treatment, and Sustainable Development

Organized by the National Polytechnic School (ENP)

Introduction

The 1st International Conference on Environment, Management, Treatment, and Sustainable Development is a unique opportunity to bring together researchers, experts, and practitioners from around the world to discuss critical issues related to environmental sustainability. Organized by the Laboratory of Environmental Sciences and Technology (LSTE) and the Water Science Research Laboratory (LRS-Eau) at the National Polytechnic School (ENP), the conference aims to foster innovative ideas, share advanced research, and develop practical solutions to address the pressing challenges of sustainable development.

Axes of the Seminar

  • Environmental Management and Sustainability
    • Strategies for sustainable resource management
    • Policies and governance in environmental conservation
    • Role of technology in enhancing environmental management
  • Water Resources and Management
    • Advanced techniques in water treatment and reuse
    • Integrated water resource management
    • Addressing water scarcity and ensuring sustainability
  • Waste Management and Pollution Control
    • Innovative solutions for waste reduction and recycling
    • Impact assessment and mitigation of industrial pollution
    • Cleaner production technologies
  • Climate Change and Adaptation
    • Climate risk assessment and mitigation strategies
    • Renewable energy and green technologies
    • Ecosystem restoration and climate adaptation
  • Sustainable Urban Development
    • Green infrastructure and urban planning
    • Reducing the ecological footprint of cities
    • Social and economic dimensions of sustainability
  • Emerging Trends in Environmental Science
    • Advances in environmental monitoring and modeling
    • Biotechnological applications for environmental conservation
    • Circular economy approaches to sustainable development
